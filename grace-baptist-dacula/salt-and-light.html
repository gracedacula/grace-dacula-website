Grace Baptist Dacula · 2026 Theme

Salt & Light

"You are the salt of the earth… You are the light of the world. A city set on a hill cannot be hidden."

Matthew 5:13–16

"You are the salt of the earth, but if salt has lost its taste, how shall its saltiness be restored? It is no longer good for anything except to be thrown out and trampled under people's feet. You are the light of the world. A city set on a hill cannot be hidden. Nor do people light a lamp and put it under a basket, but on a stand, and it gives light to all in the house. In the same way, let your light shine before others, so that they may see your good works and give glory to your Father who is in heaven."
Matthew 5:13–16 · ESV

Our 2026 Focus

What Does It
Mean?

Salt and Light is our guiding theme for 2026 — a call drawn straight from the Sermon on the Mount. In these two images, Jesus describes the identity and purpose of every believer: to preserve what is good, to add flavor and life to the world around us, and to shine with a brightness that points people to God.

This year, we want every member of Grace Baptist Dacula to embrace this calling — in our families, our neighborhoods, our workplaces, and our city.

Three Callings

How We Live It Out

Preserve

Salt preserves what is good. As followers of Jesus, we are called to stand for truth, uphold what is right, and protect the fabric of our community — even when it costs us something.

Shine

A lamp isn't lit to be hidden. God places us in our homes, workplaces, and neighborhoods intentionally. Our good works — done in His name — point people toward the Father.

Serve

Salt adds flavor — it transforms everything it touches. We are called to bring the love of Christ into every relationship and season of life, serving others with humility and joy.

Take Part

A Year of Salt & Light

Every month in 2026, Grace is stepping out to be salt and light in our community. Here's how you can join in.

January
Be Salt and Light

Launching the year with a focus on our identity as believers — called to season and illuminate every space we occupy.

February
"It Was So Sweet of You"

Community invitations — reaching out to neighbors, coworkers, and friends with intentional warmth and personal invitations to church.

March
Easter Service Invitations

The most significant Sunday of the year — invite someone to hear the Gospel. Every member, one invitation.

April
"Random Acts of Kindness"

A month-long emphasis on spontaneous, Spirit-led acts of kindness in our neighborhoods, schools, and workplaces.

This Month
May
Prayer Walk & New Neighbor Invitations

Walking our streets in prayer and delivering personal invitations to families who have recently moved into our church's neighborhood.

June
Missions Emphasis

Celebrating and supporting Grace's missionary families — learning their stories, praying for their work, and giving toward the global mission.

July
Blood Drive

Community outreach through a church-hosted blood drive — a tangible way to love our neighbors and serve those in need.

August
Teacher Appreciation

Public school outreach — blessing the teachers and staff in our local schools as they begin a new year.

September
"Coffee and Connection"

One-on-one neighbor outreach — meeting a neighbor for coffee, building a genuine relationship, and sharing God's love in person.

October
Operation Christmas Child

Global outreach through Samaritan's Purse — packing shoeboxes full of gifts and the Gospel for children around the world.

November
"Thank You for Your Service"

Veteran and first responder outreach — honoring those who serve and sacrifice, and expressing the gratitude of our church family.

December
Cocoa and Carols

Neighborhood outreach — bringing the joy of Christmas to our streets through carols, warm drinks, and the light of Christ.
